FAQs

Finance questions, answered.

The questions buyers, finance directors and operations leads actually ask before signing a finance case.

Can warehouse automation be paid monthly?

Yes. Orion can build automation projects around monthly finance routes, subject to specification and approval. This lets you compare equipment cost against labour, overtime and manual process cost — funding the automation against current operating cost rather than waiting for a heavy upfront CAPEX cycle.

What kind of equipment can be financed?

Conveyors, Helix parcel sortation, robot cells, AMRs, print and apply systems, scan tunnels, vision systems, WCS and turnkey automation projects can all be structured around a monthly finance route, subject to specification and approval.

Is finance guaranteed?

No. Finance is subject to specification, lender approval and commercial terms. Orion can help build the technical and commercial case, model the monthly finance figure against current labour cost and present the proposal — but we do not promise guaranteed approval.

How is the monthly finance figure calculated?

The figure depends on system size, finance term, interest rate and any deposit or residual structure. Orion uses standard amortisation maths to estimate a monthly figure based on a typical 60-month finance term. Final terms are confirmed during the finance approval stage.

How long does the finance approval process take?

Typically 1–3 weeks from a complete commercial proposal to formal approval, depending on lender, equipment scope and finance amount. Orion supports the technical and commercial side of the case; the finance decision sits with the lender.

Do you compare finance against current labour cost?

Yes. The starting point of every Orion finance case is the current monthly cost of running the manual operation — labour, overtime, agency cover and rework. Automation is then sized and structured to fund against that figure rather than sit on top of it.
